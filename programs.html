About the Faculty

The Faculty of Graduate Studies for Statistical Research (FGSSR) is one of Egypt's oldest and most prestigious institutions for advanced education in statistics, computer science, and related fields. For over 70 years, FGSSR has been a leader in statistical education and research.

The faculty offers a wide range of professional programs designed to provide students with the technical skills and expertise required in today's job market. With 18+ specialized programs, FGSSR prepares graduates to excel in various fields including data science, software engineering, risk management, and more.

Software Engineering Professional Master's Degree

A comprehensive program designed to develop advanced skills in software development, design, and engineering principles.

Professional Master's 2 Years Duration 39 Credit Hours

The Software Engineering Master's Program consists of two main phases:

  • Phase 1: Students must complete 21 credit hours of coursework spread across two academic semesters
  • Phase 2: Choose between Research Track (thesis) or Coursework Track (additional 21 credit hours including a project)

Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

  • Conduct scientific research in software engineering
  • Implement advanced software quality assurance techniques
  • Design and manage complex database systems
  • Develop secure information systems
  • Create cloud-based applications
  • Apply machine learning concepts

  • Midterm Work: 60% (Assignments, Quizzes, Projects, and Midterm Exams)

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our programs

What are the admission requirements?

Admission requirements vary by program but generally include a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution, official transcripts, application form, and may include interviews or entrance exams. Some programs require specific prerequisite courses for non-specialized applicants.

Are scholarships available?

Yes, Cairo University offers various scholarships based on academic merit, financial need, and special talents. International student scholarships are also available for specific programs. Contact the financial aid office for the most current information on scholarship opportunities.

What is the grading system?

For professional programs, the grading system typically consists of:

  • Midterm work (60%): Includes assignments, quizzes, projects, and midterm exams
  • Final examination (40%)
  • To pass a course, students must receive at least 50/100 overall and a minimum of 20/40 in the final exam
How long does it take to complete a program?

Program duration varies:

  • Professional Diplomas: 1 year (2 semesters)
  • Master's Degrees: 2 years minimum (4 semesters)
  • Doctoral Degrees: 3-4 years minimum

Each program has a maximum allowed duration for completion, which is specified in the program details.

Can I transfer credits from another university?

Yes, we accept transfer credits from accredited universities. Credits may be transferred if the courses are equivalent in content and level to those offered at Cairo University. The maximum number of transfer credits varies by program. Contact the admissions office for specific details about your situation.
